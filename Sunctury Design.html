A Quiet Practice

We design offices that breathe — rooms of soft daylight, raw timber, and slow green growth. Spaces that remember they are for people.

From the Studio Maren Holst Founding Principal · Sunctury Design

Our work begins with restraint. Before a single surface is specified, we listen — to the way light moves through a floorplate at four in the afternoon, to the rhythm of the people who will inhabit it, to the soft constraints of the building itself.

What follows is patient and material-led. Reclaimed oak floors, lime-washed plaster, hand-thrown ceramics, and a careful botany of plants chosen for the room they will live in. No surface is added for show; every element earns its quiet.

For Linden's new Oslo headquarters, we converted a 1962 cold-storage warehouse into a daylight-driven workplace built around a six-storey central atrium of reclaimed Norwegian pine and ninety-four mature plants. Every workstation is within seven metres of an operable window.

03 — Method

Four chapters, unhurried.

Chapter 01

Listening

Two months of dialogue with you, your team, and the building. We measure light, sound, and the way the room is already used.

Chapter 02

Drafting

Plans, sections, and material in hand. We sketch on tracing paper, build models in pine, and revise without ceremony until the drawing feels honest.

Chapter 03

Making

We work alongside our trusted contractors and the joinery workshops we have known for years. Site visits weekly, samples on the table monthly.

Chapter 04

Tending

We return — at six months, at a year, at three years — to care for the planting, the patinas, the small adjustments a working room asks for.
